
## Appeals
Students may appeal within 7 days.
The instructor documents evidence and rationale.
Unresolved cases escalate to the program director.



## **Appeals and Review**


If the use of AI in an assignment is questioned, the goal of the review process is to **understand how the work was produced**, not to assume misconduct.



### **Step 1: Instructor Review**


The instructor will first review:

- the submitted work
- any AI disclosure provided
- the assignment requirements and learning objectives

At this stage, many questions are resolved through clarification alone.


### **Step 2: Student Explanation**

If clarification is needed, you may be asked to:



- explain **how** you used AI tools (if any)
- describe your **reasoning process** and key decisions
- walk through your work (e.g., drafts, notes, code, outlines)


This conversation is about **process**, not perfection.


### **Step 3: Determination**


After review, the instructor may determine that:

- the AI use was **appropriate and properly disclosed**
- the AI use was **inappropriate but unintentional**, or
- the AI use constitutes **academic misconduct** under the Honor Code


You will be informed of the outcome and next steps.


### **Step 4: Right to Appeal**


If you disagree with the instructor’s determination, you have the right to appeal.


The appeal process follows the **standard procedures outlined in the syllabus** and may include:

- submitting a written appeal
- providing supporting materials (drafts, notes, version history)
- review by a program director, department chair, or designated committee


### **Important Notes**


- **Transparency matters.** Clear AI disclosure helps resolve questions quickly.
- **Documentation helps.** Drafts, notes, and version history can support your explanation.
- **AI detection tools alone are not determinative.** Decisions are based on evidence, context, and explanation.


### **Guiding Principle**


 **The purpose of review is fairness and learning, not punishment.**


Students are encouraged to ask questions early, document their process, and communicate openly if uncertainty arises.
