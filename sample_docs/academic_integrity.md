## **Academic Integrity**


Academic integrity means that the work you submit **honestly represents your own understanding, effort, and authorship**. This principle applies to all assignments, whether or not AI tools are used.


### **Authorship and Responsibility**


You are the author of any work you submit in this course. This means:

- the ideas, reasoning, and conclusions reflect your own understanding
- you can explain and defend the work you submit
- you take responsibility for accuracy, originality, and clarity


Using tools (including AI) does not transfer authorship or responsibility.


### **Misrepresentation of Authorship**


Misrepresentation of authorship occurs when:


- AI-generated content is submitted as if it were your own thinking
- AI is used to complete work you do not understand
- required AI use is not disclosed
- another person’s work or automated output is presented as original


Misrepresentation of authorship—whether intentional or not—is a violation of academic integrity.


### **Review and Determination**


If concerns arise:

- the instructor will review the work, assignment requirements, and any disclosures
- you may be asked to explain your process, reasoning, or development of the work
- decisions are based on **evidence, context, and explanation**, not automated tools alone


AI detection tools may be used as one input, but **they are not determinative by themselves**.


### **Appeals**


If a determination is made regarding academic integrity:

- you will be informed of the outcome
- you have the right to appeal following the procedures outlined in the syllabus
- appeals may involve review by a program director, department chair, or designated committee


### **Guiding Principle**


 **Integrity is about honest learning, not perfect work.**

Students are encouraged to ask questions, disclose tool use, and document their process. Transparency and communication protect both learning and fairness.
