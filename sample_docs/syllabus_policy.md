## **Acceptable Use of AI in This Class**



AI tools (such as ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot, or similar systems) are **encouraged** in this course when used thoughtfully and responsibly. Because AI is now part of professional practice, this class treats AI as a **learning aid**, not a shortcut.

The guiding principle is simple:

> **AI may assist your thinking, but it may not replace your thinking.**



###  Permitted Uses of AI



Unless an assignment explicitly states otherwise, you may use AI tools for:

- **Brainstorming and ideation**

  (e.g., generating initial ideas, outlining approaches, suggesting alternatives)

- **Planning and structuring work**

  (e.g., organizing arguments, creating outlines, identifying sub-problems)

- **Analysis and exploration**

  (e.g., summarizing perspectives, comparing options, testing assumptions)

- **Prototyping and drafting**

  (e.g., early drafts, sample code, pseudocode, exploratory explanations)



These uses are intended to **support learning**, not bypass it.



### Required Disclosure (Always)



When you use AI assistance, you must clearly disclose:

1. **Which tool(s)** you used
2. **How** you used them (for what purpose)
3. **What you changed, verified, or added yourself**



A short disclosure statement is sufficient. For example:

> *“I used ChatGPT to brainstorm possible frameworks for this analysis and to draft an initial outline. I revised all sections, verified factual claims, and developed the final arguments independently.”*



###  Your Responsibility as the Author



When you submit work in this class, **you are the author**, regardless of AI assistance.

That means you are responsible for:

- the **accuracy** of all claims
- the **logic and reasoning** of the argument
- the **originality** of the submission
- your ability to **explain and defend** the work



If you cannot explain how your submission works or why it is correct, that is a signal of misuse.

###  

### Prohibited Uses of AI



The following uses are **not permitted** and will be treated as academic misconduct under the Honor Code:

- Submitting AI-generated work that you do not understand
- Using AI to **misrepresent authorship** (presenting AI output as your own thinking)
- Using AI to complete an assignment **explicitly marked as no-AI**
- Using AI to evade learning objectives (e.g., bypassing required reasoning, reflection, or explanation)



Intent matters, but **outcomes matter more**. If AI replaces your learning, it is being misused.



### Gray Areas and Judgment



Not every AI use case fits neatly into “allowed” or “not allowed.”

If you are unsure:

- **Ask before submitting**
- Document your use clearly
- Err on the side of transparency



Good disclosure will never harm your grade.

Undisclosed AI use might.



###  Appeals and Review



If AI use is questioned:

- The instructor will review the work and the disclosure
- You may be asked to explain your process or reasoning
- You have the right to follow the standard **appeals process** outlined in the syllabus



### **One Sentence to Remember**



> **AI can support your work.**

> **You remain responsible for it.**
